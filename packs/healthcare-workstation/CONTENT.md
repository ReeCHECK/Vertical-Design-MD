# Healthcare Workstation Content Rules

## Terminology

Use organization-approved clinical terminology while preserving the source wording where interpretation could change meaning. Expand ambiguous abbreviations. Distinguish patient-reported, clinician-observed, device-derived, imported, and inferred information.

## Values and units

Always bind a unit to its value and prevent line wrapping that separates them ambiguously. Reference ranges include population/context and source when relevant. Do not convert or round clinical values unless a reviewed rule defines the conversion and original remains available.

## Time and authorship

Show observation time before entry time when clinical sequence matters. Include timezone in cross-site or audit contexts. Relative time is supplementary. Drafts and signed entries name author/status according to privacy policy.

## Alerts and confirmations

Alerts state the finding, possible consequence, evidence/source, and required response. Avoid alarmist language for low-certainty information. Confirmation of a high-consequence action restates patient, medication or object, intended change, and known conflict.

## Localization

Localized clinical terminology requires domain review, not generic machine translation alone. Units, decimal separators, date order, name order, address, and writing direction are independently configurable. Never translate identifiers or codes that must remain exact.
